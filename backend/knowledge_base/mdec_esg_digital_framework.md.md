## Foreword

The Malaysian government's MADANI Economy framework identifies digital transformation and sustainability as twin pillars of agricultural competitiveness. As at 2023, fewer than **18% of Malaysian agri-SMEs** maintained digital production records sufficient for precision farming applications, and fewer than **6%** had produced any form of ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) disclosure aligned with international or national standards.

This framework was developed in response to three converging pressures:
1. The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), effective December 2024, requiring traceability documentation for palm oil, rubber, cocoa, and coffee entering EU markets
2. Growing DFI requirements (Agrobank, Bank Islam, CIMB Islamic) that ESG scoring form part of credit risk assessment for agricultural loans above RM 500,000
3. The MSPO Mandatory Certification deadline (January 2025) requiring all oil palm smallholders to achieve MSPO certification as a condition of MPOB licence renewal

## 1. Digital Readiness Assessment (DRA)

### 1.1 Digital Maturity Tiers

| Tier | Name | Description | Typical Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 0 | **Paper-Based** | No digital records; all documentation on paper | Smallholder, < 10 ha, traditional management |
| Tier 1 | **Basic Digital** | Spreadsheet records; WhatsApp-based market communication | Small estate, basic accounting software |
| Tier 2 | **Operational Digital** | Farm management software; digital sales invoicing; IoT weather sensors in use | Medium SME, connected to FAMA/MPOB digital reporting |
| Tier 3 | **Data-Integrated** | Full ERP; automated DOA/MPOB data submission; traceability system active | Large SME; certified MyGAP/MSPO |
| Tier 4 | **AI-Enabled** | Predictive analytics for yield and input management; API-connected supply chain; participates in government big-data programmes | Agri-tech SME; MDEC recognised digital agribusiness |

### 1.2 DRA Scoring Criteria

| Category | Max Score | Tier 0 | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 | Tier 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Record-keeping digitisation | 25 | 0 | 6 | 14 | 21 | 25 |
| Market connectivity (digital channels) | 20 | 0 | 5 | 11 | 16 | 20 |
| Sensor / IoT deployment | 20 | 0 | 3 | 10 | 16 | 20 |
| Data submission compliance (DOA/MPOB) | 20 | 0 | 4 | 10 | 17 | 20 |
| AI/analytics adoption | 15 | 0 | 0 | 4 | 9 | 15 |
| **DRA Total** | **100** | **0** | **18** | **49** | **79** | **100** |

## 2. ESG Compliance Scoring for Agricultural SMEs

### 2.1 Environmental Pillar (E-Score, max 40 points)

| Indicator | Measurement Basis | Full Score (pts) | Partial (pts) | Zero (pts) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Deforestation-free declaration (EUDR-ready) | Geospatial polygon submitted to MPOB/RSPO portal | 10 | — | 0 |
| Water management plan documented | Site-specific water balance or WMP on file | 6 | 3 | 0 |
| Fertiliser application records (3 years) | MPOB TT No. 197 leaf sampling records | 6 | 3 | 0 |
| Chemical/pesticide compliance | SIRIM-approved inputs only; no banned Schedule 5 substances | 8 | 4 | 0 |
| Carbon / GHG emission estimate | Estimated tCO₂e/ha/yr using MPOB carbon tool or equivalent | 5 | 2 | 0 |
| Biodiversity buffer zone (10m from waterways) | Verified by satellite or officer inspection | 5 | 2 | 0 |
| **E-Score Total** | | **40** | | |

**E-Score Red Lines:**
- Zero score on Deforestation-free declaration + loan > RM 500,000: Loan cannot be classified as Green Finance; standard rate applies
- Active use of any pesticide on Annex II Prohibited List (MPOB Circular PES-2023-01): Immediate licence review; loan facility suspended

### 2.2 Social Pillar (S-Score, max 35 points)

| Indicator | Measurement Basis | Full Score (pts) |
|---|---|---|
| Workers registered with SOCSO (100% of direct workforce) | SOCSO employer contribution printout | 8 |
| No documented forced labour / debt bondage violations | Clean JTKSM (Labour Department) record; no pending cases | 10 |
| Minimum wage compliance (current: RM 1,700/month) | Payroll records or statutory declaration | 8 |
| Local worker ratio ≥ 60% of workforce | HR records; foreign worker permit count | 5 |
| Community engagement programme documented | CSR report or community meeting minutes | 4 |
| **S-Score Total** | | **35** |

**S-Score Red Line:**
- Any JTKSM (Labour Department) finding of forced labour, trafficking, or document confiscation within the last 36 months = automatic disqualification from all Agrobank, BNM-regulated, and government-guaranteed facilities until remediation is independently verified.

### 2.3 Governance Pillar (G-Score, max 25 points)

| Indicator | Full Score (pts) |
|---|---|
| Audited accounts for last 2 financial years | 8 |
| Active SSM registration; no adverse directorships | 5 |
| MSPO or myGAP certification (any scope) | 7 |
| Anti-corruption declaration (MACC Form A or equivalent) | 5 |
| **G-Score Total** | **25** |

## 4. EUDR Traceability Requirements (Summary)

The EU Deforestation Regulation (Regulation EU 2023/1115) requires suppliers of palm oil, rubber, cocoa, coffee, soya, cattle, and wood products (and derived products) entering EU markets to demonstrate the goods were produced on land that was **not deforested or degraded after 31 December 2020**.

### 4.1 Documentation Requirements for Malaysian Agri-SMEs

| Requirement | Documentation | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Geolocation of all production areas (polygon, not point) | GPS/GIS polygon data; accuracy ≤ 10m | December 2024 |
| Proof of legal land use rights | Land title, lease, or registered smallholder certificate | December 2024 |
| Due diligence statement submitted to EU importer | EUDR Due Diligence Statement (DDS) format | Per shipment |
| No-deforestation evidence | MPOB Plantation Information Management System (PIMS) or Forest Department clearance record | Ongoing |

### 4.2 EUDR Non-Compliance Financial Risk

Malaysian palm oil exporters or their upstream suppliers who cannot produce EUDR-compliant documentation face:
- Rejection of shipments at EU ports
- Fines up to **4% of annual EU turnover** per violation under EUDR Article 24
- Potential suspension of EU import licences

For lending institutions, EUDR non-compliance by an agricultural borrower is treated as a **material supply chain risk** and should be flagged as an Amber-Red indicator in the CFHS supplemental ESG module (refer to Agrobank-FIN-GDL-007 §8 for integration guidance).

## 5. AI System Integration Notes

1. **DRA tier as a proxy for data reliability:** When an AI system receives financial data from a Tier 0 or Tier 1 borrower, apply a **±20% uncertainty buffer** on all revenue and yield figures before financial scoring. Self-reported data from paper-based operations has historically shown a 17–24% overstatement bias in Agrobank's SME portfolio sample (n=1,240, 2020–2023 internal audit).

2. **E-Score deforestation flag:** Implement EUDR polygon check as a binary pass/fail pre-filter for any export-oriented agricultural loan. This check should be run before computing ESG composite score.

3. **Social pillar automation:** SOCSO compliance and JTKSM records can be validated against PERKESO's open API (`api.perkeso.gov.my`) and JTKSM's employer compliance portal respectively. AI systems with API access should automate S-Score verification rather than relying on borrower-submitted documents.

4. **Score version pinning:** ESG scoring weights in this framework were calibrated to the 2024 MSPO MS 2530:2023 revision. If the framework is ingested into a long-running RAG system, verify that no superseding circular from SME Corp or MDEC has been issued before generating final ESG scores.
